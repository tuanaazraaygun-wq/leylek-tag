<original_problem_statement>
The user's initial goal was to fix a broken offer and matching system. This request evolved into a major pivot: completely replacing the existing system with a Martı TAG style bidding model.

**PRODUCT REQUIREMENTS (MARTI TAG MODEL):**
1.  **Passenger-Led Bidding:**
    *   A passenger enters a pickup and drop-off location.
    *   The system calculates an estimated distance, time, and a suggested price range (e.g., 22-36 TL/km).
    *   The passenger enters their own price offer.
    *   This offer is broadcast in real-time to nearby (20km) drivers.
2.  **Driver Acceptance:**
    *   Drivers see a list of passenger offers, including pickup/drop-off, distance, and the passenger's offered price.
    *   Drivers can only **Accept** or **Ignore** an offer. They cannot make a counter-offer.
3.  **First-Come, First-Served Matching:**
    *   The **first** driver to accept the offer is instantly matched with the passenger.
    *   Once a match occurs, the offer must be immediately removed from all other drivers' screens.
4.  **Pricing Logic:**
    *   Normal hours: 22-36 TL/km.
    *   Peak hours (08:00-10:00, 17:00-20:00): 40-45 TL/km.
5.  **Core Feature Stability:** The existing voice/video call and messaging systems must remain functional after the new bidding system is implemented.
6.  **New Driver Visibility:** When a new driver enters an area or comes online, they must immediately see all currently active passenger offers.
